About

Hey there! I am an Eagle Scout and Computer Science major with a minor in German at the University of Montana. I am a member of the Davidson Honors College and the Franke Global Leadership Initiative. I enjoy music, theater, transit design, being outside, and learning about the world.

I have a deep love for technology, old and new, useful and... well, not. I'm not afraid to step out of my comfort zone, if I need to. I pull apart, break, fix, and put stuff back together for fun. If you asked me how many pieces of technology I've destroyed over the years, I wouldn't be able to tell you — but I've probably fixed just as many!

I work in IT in a primarily Windows household, but we do have our fair share of Macs managed through Jamf.

I am also a fierce supporter of urbanism and human-centric, bike-centric, and transit-centric city design. I try to be politically active, when my life allows it.

I am a musician and thespian. I played cello for seven years and am in a student-led a Cappella choir. I was in my high school's theatre program for three years running sound and lights. I've since volunteered at the Missoula Children's theatre on the sound and front-of-house teams for a few shows here and there.

I am currently learning Java, Bash, and Django.
